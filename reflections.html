R2: Introduction to Design Thinking Process

When it comes to the design thinking process, well, like any other process, we think that it would contain logical steps until the end. It is just with this one, it is kind of spontaneous because you would be jumping from different steps from time to time. You could either go back from the first one or retrace steps to further enhance the entirety of the project. Moreover, every step is a building block for the other. I also appreciate the Design thinking mindsets that one must contain and encourage, because it would be an overwhelming process to be able to finish.

The first thing that comes to my mind and it is also the most important step for me, is the part where we understand our user. The project is for them and to be able to address their problems and pose solutions, I think is the most important thing that we need to understand while we decide what and how to design. Therefore, empathy is a such a strong feat because it helps us jump into the lives of the people we are trying to help. I may not do much and my skills would might be low for now, but my eagerness I mean our eagerness I feel, is the greatest push we could ever have to be able to accomplish the project. Another important thing is for us to muster up optimism, embrace ambiguity and just make it. For me, like I have said, I am not the best with my technical skills, but that should not limit on what I must accomplish. I need to just say yes to possibilities and what I can do and what I can contribute and what I feel would help my users. It is kind of like, the intentions should be there and then the skills come after. Your ideas are what will inspire for you to move out of the line. Just do it and when in doubt, it is alright to have mistakes. Learn from failure because this is where we grow. An example I have experience with this is when me and my team mates where throwing off ideas everywhere and while it is good, we were raising a lot of problems that we forget where to focus on and what to actually cater that is why our project proposal were a bit confusing. Then we learn from all the criticisms we got from our classmates and teachers. Now we have a clearer goal of everything and started to have direction. And although it is still not the best we could come up with, it only means that there would always be room for changes and polishing. That is why we must iterate our work as much as we can until we can come to a stage where we will be confident at achieving the standards that stemmed from our ideas.

To conclude, design thinking process, cannot be done by just yourself and in one sitting. One must go through a series of process, which sometimes might even be complicated at times, but we should trust the process and offer the best that we can. It is a process where we understand users, brainstorm ideas, creation of the idea, designing prototypes and doing the actual thing. And along the lines, we might even create even more processes beneath it, whatever works for the team. Afterall, design process matters on how it is being handled and not because it was served through a manual.

R4: Information Architecture

This topic was helpful because we were introduced to different types of website, how they were arranged based on needs, and how information is organized. Through my experiences as a user, there were times where I will be in a website wherein it is hard to navigate through. What always happen at the end of course is I exit it. This is because I can only spare much of my time, I do not have forever, and the internet offers too much. We would not want to be stuck on a single page taking forever to figure out where the contents you are looking for is placed.

Understanding Information Architecture not only helps organize data but also how it can and be presented. The time and effort you need to dedicate to perfect it is fruitful. To be able to understand such thing is also to be able to understand people. How their mind will work, how their eyes will wander, what would they be looking for, and where can they go to next, are the questions(and a whole lot more) are what we need to be pondering through a lot. And it takes a great amount of time and effort. It took hundreds of research and data gathering to be able to have websites that are functional and convenient nowadays. It has made me more appreciative of all the knowledge that has been put just so people can have an easier time. Also, with information architecture, we are organizing data therefore we are not only giving ease to the users but also to the creators.

The main takeaway I got from this lesson is that information architecture is an integral aspect of designing. This is where we truly need to understand what will work best for the users. A good design can never be achieved without a good information architecture otherwise it would not make sense. The dedication of research and studying makes us ready for the planning process that is why it was a great topic to be learned.

R5: Visual Information and Design

Traversing to a deeper and more specific topics about design, we start to analyze even the smallest details and how it can affect and make the slightest (or even the biggest) of change. Most people would not even think of these things and it was interesting that we get to learn about it. I am a huge fan of designing and art and all that, that is why it was interesting, and I have truly enjoyed it. The fact that we are studying about the 'aesthetics' part of design makes me ecstatic.

Moreover, visual information design makes importance of three aspects namely: typography, layout, and color. There was this video that was assigned for us to watch and it was about how a better typography can change anything. It was interesting since some events were listed most of which mistakes that occurred during awards show. And to be honest before I have watched it, I have always thought that it was the announcer's fault for saying the wrong things because how hard can it be to read something? Then I realized after watching the video that I was wrong. Bad design can make the reader feel the burden that it is their mistake and that would be all the mistake of the designer for misleading its reader. To think that a simple typographical mistake can impact hugely to people's career or even lives, say something a lot about it.

Another thing is the layout. I remembered that we have an activity that plays with the layout of a website called "userinyerface" and I remembered seriously having hard time finishing it that it took me 19 minutes before I was done. It was embarrassing because when you look at others time, we would see that their time was less. My point is layout is very powerful to the point that it cannot only affect how the user can feel now, but also moments after. As a user at that time, going through the website with such ridiculous layout of course made me stress, angry and confused. Not only at that moment while I was using it, but I also felt embarrassment moments after. And that is with just the impact of the layout. Other scenarios can be for example, a user goes to this website where it displays pictures and its contents very widely on the screen. What this can do is that the user can feel insecure assuming that he/she is using a desktop computer in a public place and wants the contents to be for himself only.

Last is the color. We can see colors of different designs every day from posters, videos, games, websites, clothes, cars even houses. And just because something is colorful, does not mean is pleasing in the eye. Some colors are not just meant to be together and it can affect how people can perceive the design. It is not just a matter of picking all the good colors and combining them all to look good but rather to have a good single color then find other colors that can complement that color and that is why it is important to have a good set of palette.
